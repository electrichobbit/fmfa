    Section 7.4 Bond Amortization

    When we studied loans, we amortized them to figure out things like how much of principal was repaid in a payment, or what the outstanding balance was at the time of any payment. Based on our analogy comparing bonds to loans, we can do the same with bonds - in fact, we often have to do so for tax purposes. But the analogy will need some ... interpretation.
    🔗

    Activity 7.4.1.

    We start with some examples: a premium bond, a par bond, and a discount bond. We will assume \(F = C\) and that coupons are paid annually (this keeps the computations simple and allows us to see the patterns).
    🔗
    In the tables that follow, the payment is simply the coupon payment \(Fr\) for all payments except the last, where the payment is \(F+Fr\) (to include redemption of the face amount). The outstanding balance at time 0 is the price of the bond. The interest due is the yield rate \(\iota\) times the previous outstanding balance. The principal repaid is what is left of the payment after accounting for interest, and the outstanding balance is the previous outstanding balance minus the principal repaid.

    The AMORT worksheet will also work for bonds, with a couple of caveats: (1) Keep in mind the sign conventions: for loans, interest and principal repaid are negative in the financial calculator, but we report them with opposite signs, and (2) the calculator will not include the redemption amount in the last payment -- that will show in the outstanding balance.

    Before going on, we introduce some definitions and notation.
    🔗
    • At time \(t\text{,}\) the outstanding balance of the bond is called the Book Value and denoted \(B_t\text{.}\) Note that \(B_0 = P\text{,}\) the price of the bond.
      🔗
      🔗
    • If the bond is priced at a premium, it is the premium that is amortized. We call that amortization of premium or writing down the bond.
      🔗
      🔗
    • If the bond is priced at a discount, it is the discount that is amortized. We call that accumulation of discount or writing up the bond.
